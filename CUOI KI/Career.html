CATEGORY: WHO?

Lee Sang-hyeok: The Unkillable Demon King

Born on May 7, 1996, in Seoul, South Korea, Lee Sang-hyeok, better known by his in-game name "Faker," is widely regarded as the greatest League of Legends player of all time. From a young age, Faker exhibited an exceptional talent for video games, spending countless hours honing his skills and solving intricate puzzles.

A Commoner’s Beginnings

Lee Sang-hyeok, famously known as Faker, had a childhood steeped in curiosity and a natural affinity for games. Born on May 7, 1996, in Seoul, South Korea, he was the youngest of two siblings. Despite his parents' divorce and the financial challenges that followed, Faker's ingenuity shone through. Raised primarily by his father and grandparents, he found solace and joy in puzzles and video games from an early age. His love for problem-solving extended to creating custom maps for games like Warcraft III and Chaos. Interestingly enough, quite a few of the world’s top professional LoL players have a background in Warcraft 3, the game which spawned the original DotA. When playing WC3, Faker spent quite a bit of time in custom maps – most notably a map called Chaos, an early MOBA game similar to LoL. Faker claims that he was “extremely good” at Chaos, and seeing his success as a professional League of Legends player he likely wasn’t wrong.

Enter: League of Legends

Faker began to play League of Legends in December 2011 “by mere coincidence”, as he has said to have found the game while browsing the web. Playing on the Korean server under the alias “GoJeonPa”, Faker quickly gained notoriety. Many players speculated about just who the player who had managed to reach #1 on the ranked ladder was, with suggestions ranging from GoJeonPa being a smurf to being a foreign pro on the Korean server.
“Honestly, I didn’t really know I was that good…” – Faker
In late 2012, Faker received contact from Korean eSports organization SK Telecom T1 regarding potentially picking him up. After some thought, Faker decided to become a professional gamer. Luckily enough, his parents were completely on board with his decision to follow his dreams. Faker showed promise, but who would have predicted that within months, he would be recognized as the best League of Legends player in the world.

Early success

In 2013, at just 17 years old, Faker joined SK Telecom T1 (now T1) as their mid-laner and quickly made his mark. His debut year was spectacular, leading SKT to win the LCK Summer Split and the 2013 World Championship. A defining moment was his iconic solo kill against Ambition, another top mid-laner, during the OGN Champions Summer Finals. This fearless play showcased his exceptional mechanical skill and strategic mind, cementing his reputation as a prodigy. The solo kill against Ambition became legendary, highlighting his potential to dominate the game and outplay even the most skilled opponents. This early success set the stage for his continued dominance and legendary status in the world of League of Legends.

Downfall

From 2017 to 2022, Faker faced several challenges and setbacks that marked a period of struggle in his career. In 2017, despite reaching the World Championship finals, SKT T1 lost to Samsung Galaxy, ending their dynasty. The following years saw Faker and his team struggle to reclaim their dominance, with inconsistent performances in both the LCK and international tournaments. The period from 2018 to 2021 was particularly tough, as SKT/T1 failed to win any World Championships during this time. The team faced internal issues, roster changes, and intense competition from other top teams. However, these challenges also served as a catalyst for Faker's growth and resilience, eventually leading to his triumphant return to form in 2023 with a fifth World Championship title.

Emperor return

Faker's comeback story is one of grit, determination, and unrivaled talent. After a challenging period from 2017 to 2022, where he and his team faced inconsistent performances and internal struggles, Faker's resilience began to shine through. The turning point came in 2023 when T1, fueled by a mix of experienced veterans and promising new talents, started to regain their footing in the competitive scene. Faker's exceptional leadership and unwavering skill played a crucial role in T1's resurgence. His ability to adapt to new metas, continuously hone his mechanical prowess, and mentor younger teammates helped the team rebuild their synergy and confidence. This culminated in a spectacular run during the 2023 World Championship, where Faker's strategic genius and clutch performances led T1 to reclaim the coveted title. The victory in 2023 marked his fifth World Championship win, but Faker wasn't done yet. In 2024, he led T1 to yet another triumphant victory, securing his sixth World Championship title. This back-to-back success solidified his legacy as the greatest player in League of Legends history. Faker's comeback is a testament to his enduring dedication, proving that even in the face of adversity, true champions can rise again, stronger than ever.
